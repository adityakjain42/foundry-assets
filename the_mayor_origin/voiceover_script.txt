I learned power in a room where no one raised their voice.
The Empire did not fall from war. It fell from paperwork, hunger, and late ships.
Traders bought the routes. Scholars priced the rumors. Intuits moved through the fold and left softer thoughts behind.
My first treason was only a theorem: a stipend for silence costs less than cavalry.
Then the jump reports arrived. Ships missing. Voices returning wrong. The Palace called it safe.
So I made a scandal useful. Panic became policy. Policy became a ballot.
I detest politics. Truly. But the people had already made up their minds.
Violence is the last refuge of the incompetent, Empress. The Era Frontier needs a hand on the ledger, not a fist on the throat. And I, though I detest power, am to be its Mayor.
